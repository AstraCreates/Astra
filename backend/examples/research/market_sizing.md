Defensible market sizing. ALWAYS show the math and cite the inputs. Prefer bottom-up.
Mark any number you couldn't source as "UNVERIFIED" — do not fabricate.

## Definitions
- TAM (Total Addressable Market): everyone who could ever buy the category.
- SAM (Serviceable Available Market): the slice you can reach (geo, segment, channel).
- SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market): what you can realistically win in 1–3 yrs.

## Bottom-up (preferred — credible)
TAM = ({{number_of_target_accounts}}) × ({{annual_contract_value}})
SAM = TAM × ({{% reachable: geo/segment/regulatory}})
SOM = SAM × ({{realistic_share_yr1-3, e.g. 1–5%}})

Show every input with a source:
- # of target accounts: {{value}} — source: {{census/industry report/directory}}
- ACV: {{value}} — source: {{your pricing or comparable pricing}}
- Reachable %: {{value}} — rationale: {{geo/segment limits}}

## Top-down (sanity check only)
Industry size {{$X}} (source: {{report}}) × your category % × your reachable %.
Use to CHECK the bottom-up number, not as the primary figure.

## Output
- TAM $: ___  | SAM $: ___ | SOM (yr1–3) $: ___
- 2-line narrative: who the buyer is, why the SOM is achievable.
- Sensitivity: show SOM at conservative / base / optimistic share.

Rules:
- A figure with no traceable input is worthless to an investor — cite or label UNVERIFIED.
- Bottom-up first. "It's a $50B market" top-down alone reads as hand-waving.
