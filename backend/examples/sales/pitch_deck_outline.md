Two variants. Replace `{{...}}`. One idea per slide; the deck supports the talk, not vice versa.

## A) SALES deck (selling to a customer)
1. Title — {{product}}: {{one_line_value_prop}}
2. The problem — the status quo + what it costs {{ICP}}.
3. Why now — the trigger/shift making this urgent.
4. The solution — what {{product}} is, in one sentence + one visual.
5. How it works — 3 steps.
6. Outcomes — the metrics customers get (before→after).
7. Proof — logos, testimonial with a number, mini case study.
8. Differentiation — vs {{alternative}}: the 2–3 things only you do.
9. Pricing — packages, anchored to ROI not cost.
10. Implementation — onboarding timeline, what's required of them.
11. Next step — the specific ask (pilot/contract/start date).

## B) FUNDRAISE deck (selling to an investor)
1. Title + one-liner. 2. Problem. 3. Solution. 4. Why now.
5. Market size (TAM/SAM/SOM). 6. Product (demo/screens).
7. Business model. 8. Traction (the chart that matters — revenue/users/retention).
9. Competition (2x2 or feature matrix). 10. Team. 11. The ask + use of funds.

Rules:
- Slide titles are sentences that state the takeaway ("Onboarding takes 2 days, not 2 months"), not labels ("Onboarding").
- Lead with outcome/traction; bury the feature tour.
- Numbers on every proof slide.
