A 30-minute discovery framework. Goal: qualify + diagnose, NOT pitch. Talk <40% of the time.
Replace `{{...}}`.

## 1. Open (2 min)
"Thanks for the time, {{name}}. I've got us 30 min — I want to understand {{their_world}}
and see if {{product}} is even a fit. If it's not, I'll tell you. Sound fair?"
Agenda check + permission to ask questions.

## 2. Current state (8 min) — diagnose
- "Walk me through how you handle {{job_to_be_done}} today."
- "What tools / process are you using now?"
- "How long does {{key_workflow}} take you?"
- "Who else touches this?"

## 3. Pain + impact (8 min) — quantify
- "What made you take this call now?" (trigger event)
- "What's that costing you — time, money, missed {{outcome}}?"
- "What happens if it stays the way it is for another 6 months?"
- (Quantify: $ / hours / risk. No number = weak deal.)

## 4. Desired future + decision (7 min)
- "If this were solved, what would change for you?"
- "Who needs to be involved to make a change like this?"
- "What's your timeline / budget range for fixing this?"
- "What would need to be true for you to move forward?"

## 5. Next step (5 min)
Recap their pain in their words → confirm fit → propose a concrete next step
(tailored demo, pilot, proposal) with a date on the calendar before hanging up.

Qualify against (MEDDIC-lite): Metrics, Economic buyer, Decision criteria,
Decision process, Pain, Champion. Note gaps as follow-ups.

Rules:
- Ask, don't pitch. Every claim should answer a pain they stated.
- Always book the next step live; "I'll follow up" = dead deal.
