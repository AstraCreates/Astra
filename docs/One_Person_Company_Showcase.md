# Astra: The One-Person Company

All you need is the idea, and Astra brings the company.

Astra is built for the founder who wants to move like a team without hiring one first. Give it a goal and Astra turns that goal into a coordinated operating run: it plans the work, dispatches specialist agents, streams progress live, produces deliverables, stores company memory, and keeps risky actions behind approvals.

This doc is written for landing pages, pitch decks, accelerator applications, demo scripts, and founder-facing product storytelling.

## The Short Version

Astra is an AI company-building platform.

It is not just a chatbot, and it is not just an agent wrapper. It is a system for turning a single founder into a functioning company engine with memory, execution, approvals, deliverables, and repeatable operating structure.

If you want the positioning in one line:

**Astra turns a founder's idea into a staffed company.**

## Why It Feels Different

- **It starts from outcomes, not prompts.** A founder gives Astra a goal, and Astra converts that goal into a run with agents, context, and artifacts.
- **It behaves like an org chart.** Research, legal, design, technical, marketing, sales, finance, ops, and web all exist as dedicated specialist surfaces.
- **It keeps company memory.** Sessions, digests, workboards, vault files, and context-aware records mean the company gets smarter over time.
- **It is built for real execution.** Approvals, integrations, deployments, billing hooks, and observability are part of the product, not side features.
- **It is friendly to solo founders.** The product story is simple: one person can operate with the leverage of a whole company.

## Feature Map

### 1. Goal-to-Run Orchestration

The core product action is `POST /goal`.

That is where Astra receives a founder objective and turns it into a durable session. From there it streams progress in real time, dispatches the right agents, records outputs, and keeps the work organized until completion.

What this means in plain English:

- the founder gives Astra a business objective
- Astra figures out how to execute it
- work happens across multiple specialist agents
- progress is visible while it runs
- the result is stored, replayable, and inspectable

This is the part that makes Astra feel less like a tool and more like an operating system for the business.

### 2. Specialist Agent Team

Astra has a broad specialist bench, including:

- legal
- web

- design

- infra
- data

This matters for storytelling because it lets you say Astra does not just answer a question. It assembles the right functional operators around the problem.

### 3. Agent Stack Platform

Astra compiles business outcomes into deployable execution packages.

That means it can take a goal and turn it into a structured stack with:

- lanes of work
- approval gates
- artifact expectations
- connector requirements
- quality checks
- readiness checks
- operating plans
- execution blueprints

This is a strong deck feature because it reframes Astra as infrastructure for AI departments, not a one-off agent demo.

### 4. Company Memory and Context

Astra keeps durable context across sessions and workspaces.

The product stores and reuses:

- session history
- digests
- workboards
- company reports
- subteam reports
- vault files
- attachments
- context-aware memory

In pitch language: Astra remembers the company, not just the conversation.

### 5. Workspaces, Chapters, and Ongoing Structure

Every goal can live inside a wider founder workspace.

That gives Astra a stronger narrative than single-run tools:

- related work stays grouped
- sessions do not disappear after completion
- the founder can build a body of work over time
- company history becomes navigable instead of fragmented

For a solo founder, this is the difference between scattered AI help and an actual operating system.

### 6. Missions and Company Goals

Astra supports persistent operating goals, tasks, approvals, scheduled follow-up runs, and Notion sync.

That means the platform is not limited to one-off output generation. It can help a founder maintain an ongoing execution cadence around the business itself.

This is useful in a pitch because it suggests Astra can support the entire company journey:

- launch
- iterate
- follow up
- revisit
- move the next milestone forward

### 7. Custom Agents

Founders can define their own agents with:

- selector-based tool catalogs
- scheduled runs
- automatic result delivery

This is powerful for the “one person company” story because it allows the founder to build a bespoke internal team tuned to their business model.

You can position this as:

**Astra ships the core company, and founders can add their own specialists on top.**

### 8. Deliverables and Founder-Facing Output

Astra does not stop at internal reasoning. It produces artifacts.

Those include:

- PDFs
- TXT reports
- vault-stored files
- run summaries
- email delivery flows

That makes the product easier to sell because the output is tangible. It creates the kind of artifacts people can share with partners, teammates, investors, or operators.

### 9. Skills as Reusable Capability

Founders can attach reusable skills to agents.

This gives the platform a modular quality: the company can gain specific capabilities over time instead of requiring every task to start from scratch.

### 10. Credits and Billing Hooks

Astra includes token and credit accounting, checkout flows, webhook support, and plan-based limits.

That is important for a startup story because it means Astra is not just an internal tool. It has the scaffolding for a product that can charge, meter usage, and support business growth.

### 11. Deployments and Publishing

Astra keeps track of staging and production deployments and exposes publish endpoints for generated projects.

That gives you a clean external story:

- Astra can ship them

Astra includes founder and team access controls, invites, organization usage, and optional auth boundaries.

That matters for pitch and enterprise conversations because it shows the product is built around real ownership, not just anonymous chat sessions.

### 14. Production Operations

Astra also includes health, readiness, metrics, smoke checks, launch verification, and admin observability.

This is a useful line in a deck:

**Astra is designed to run a company, and it is instrumented like production software.**

## The Data Model Behind The Story

The product's internal structure reinforces the one-person-company narrative.

Core records include:

- founders
- goals
- tasks
- approvals
- memory documents

That is exactly the kind of structure you want when you are pitching an AI company-building platform: the system keeps track of who owns the company, what the company is trying to do, what work has been assigned, what needs approval, and what the company has learned.
