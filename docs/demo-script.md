# Astra Demo — "Victor's First Hire"

**Runtime ~2:00 · narration (say) + screen (show)**

---

### Beat 1 — The character + his pain (0:00–0:18)

*Screen: not Astra. Guy at laptop, late night, coffee, 12 tabs.*

> "This is Victor. Three weeks ago he quit his job to build a company. No team. Just him.
>
> Tonight's list: incorporate, research three competitors, draft a landing page, start a sales pipeline. Each one's a full day. Forty of them. He doesn't need answers — he needs the work **done**."

### Beat 2 — The inciting sentence (0:18–0:30)

*Screen: Astra goal box. Victor types.*

> "So he writes what he wants, not how: *'Validate my market and get me ready to launch.'* And hits go."

*Type it live. Hit Enter. Don't explain the box.*

### Beat 3 — Work happens, and it already knows him (0:30–1:05)

*Screen: run streams — plan, agents fan out in lanes. Research cites his own notes; design pulls his brand.*

> "Astra doesn't reply. It makes a plan and puts a **team** on it — a team that already knows the place, because Astra remembers everything about Victor's company in one shared brain.
>
> Research tears his competitors apart against *his* product. Design drafts the page in *his* voice. Sales builds the pipeline around the customers *he's* really after. Victor watches his company get built in real time."

### Beat 4 — He can steer it (1:05–1:30)

*Screen: Victor types into the run mid-flight — "focus the research on enterprise, not SMB." The running squad adjusts; it doesn't restart.*

> "Then he sees something and changes his mind. He just tells it — *'focus on enterprise, not small business.'*
>
> And the team already working shifts right there. No restart, no starting over — they just hear him and keep going in the new direction. Victor's not running a tool. He's steering a team that's already in motion."

### Beat 5 — It doesn't stop (1:30–1:45)

*Screen: run settles into an ongoing operating view — pipeline filling, automations ticking on their own.*

> "And that team stays in motion. When tonight's list is done, Astra doesn't clock out — the pipeline keeps filling, the work keeps running tomorrow, while Victor sleeps."

### Beat 6 — Payoff + close on the user (1:45–2:00)

*Screen: Library — market brief, entity, live landing URL, populated pipeline. Cut back to Victor closing the laptop.*

> "Twenty minutes in, Victor has a market brief, a real company, a live landing page, a pipeline with real names. He still makes every call — he's just not alone anymore.
>
> That's Astra — **the company that runs with you.**"
